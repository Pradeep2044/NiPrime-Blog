Bihar's First Sports University Gets UGC Recognition

Published on: January 11, 2025

Bihar's first sports university has achieved a significant milestone by gaining recognition from the University Grants Commission (UGC), marking a pivotal step in advancing sports education in the state. Located in Patna, this newly established institution aims to nurture athletic talent and promote academic excellence in sports sciences and management. The UGC’s recognition ensures that degrees awarded by the university will hold national credibility, attracting students from across the country and boosting Bihar’s educational profile.


The university will offer a wide range of undergraduate, postgraduate, and diploma programs tailored to meet the growing demand for skilled professionals in sports coaching, physiotherapy, sports journalism, and event management. It will also provide specialized training facilities for athletes, fostering their development through a combination of rigorous academic curriculum and state-of-the-art infrastructure. This initiative aligns with the government’s vision of making Bihar a hub for sports excellence.


Experts believe the establishment of this sports university will have a transformative impact on the state’s sports ecosystem, encouraging young athletes to pursue careers in sports without leaving Bihar. It is expected to promote grassroots-level talent identification and development, giving students access to world-class coaching and mentorship. Additionally, collaborations with national and international sports bodies are being explored to enhance the quality of education and training.


The recognition by the UGC is a significant step toward empowering the youth of Bihar and building a vibrant sports culture in the region. This development also reflects the government’s commitment to bridging regional disparities in education and sports. As the university gears up to welcome its first batch of students, it is poised to become a beacon of hope for aspiring athletes and sports professionals in the state and beyond.
