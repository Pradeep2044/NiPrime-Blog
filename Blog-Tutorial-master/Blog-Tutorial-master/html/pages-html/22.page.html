What would it take for Donald Trump to buy Greenland?

Published on: January 11, 2025

The idea of Donald Trump purchasing Greenland, which he publicly floated during his presidency, would require overcoming significant political, legal, and practical hurdles. Greenland is an autonomous territory under the sovereignty of Denmark, and any transfer of ownership would require approval from both the Danish government and the Greenlandic parliament. The people of Greenland, who have a distinct cultural and national identity, would also need to consent to such a move, as it would significantly impact their governance and future autonomy.


Financially, acquiring Greenland would involve a monumental investment, given its strategic location, vast natural resources, and geopolitical importance in the Arctic region. The United States would likely have to offer a substantial sum to Denmark, not just to cover the territory’s estimated value but also to address the political and social ramifications. Additionally, long-term commitments to infrastructure development, resource management, and ensuring the well-being of Greenland’s residents would be essential components of any agreement.


Diplomatically, a proposal to buy Greenland could strain U.S. relations with Denmark, the European Union, and other global allies. Denmark has repeatedly affirmed that Greenland is not for sale, viewing the territory as an integral part of its national sovereignty. Pushing for such a deal might be perceived as an affront to Denmark’s authority and could result in broader geopolitical tensions, particularly with nations like Russia and China, which have interests in the Arctic region.


Moreover, such a transaction would require extensive domestic support within the United States. Congress would need to approve the financial and legal frameworks for the purchase, and there would likely be public debates over the justification and benefits of such an acquisition. While Trump’s interest in Greenland reflects the strategic importance of the Arctic, turning the idea into reality would necessitate overcoming numerous legal, financial, and diplomatic obstacles, making it an extraordinarily complex proposition.
